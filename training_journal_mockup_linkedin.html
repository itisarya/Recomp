I got tired of fitness apps that feel like confetti explosions. So I built my own.

No subscriptions. No gamified streaks with fire emojis. No cloud sign-in.
Just a single HTML file — bookmarked on my iPhone — that tracks a 26-week body recomp challenge like a proper training journal should.

Here's what's inside 👇

📋 Today tab — four ring charts for adherence, gym, swim & diet. Tap to check off. Sparklines show 14-day trends at a glance.

⚖️ Weight tab — log daily, watch the trend curve bend downward, track the delta to goal.

🔥 Streaks — a GitHub-style heatmap of every day you showed up, plus current streak counters that make skipping feel genuinely costly.

📐 Setup wizard — name your challenge, set your dates, define step goals. Everything else flows from there.

The aesthetic is deliberately editorial — think Kinfolk meets a training log. Fraunces serif, warm cream background, sage green accents. Because the tools you actually use shouldn't be ugly.

Tech stack: vanilla HTML/CSS/JS. LocalStorage persistence. Zero dependencies (Chart.js for the weight graph, that's it). Netlify deploy in 30 seconds. No backend, no auth, no cost.

The whole thing is ~1,800 lines. Built in a weekend. Used every single day since.

If you've ever wanted a fitness tracker that respects your aesthetic intelligence — build one. It's more motivating than anything in the App Store.
